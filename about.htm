About Us

Peace Through Language is a research and engagement initiative focused on understanding, measuring, and transforming harmful online language into constructive, peace‑oriented communication. We design tools, corpora, and public engagement activities to help communities moderate content, foster empathy, and reduce harm.

Dr. Phoebe Teh (Phoey Lee Teh)

Reader in Social Computing

Phoey Lee Teh leads the Peace Through Language initiative, working at the intersection of computational linguistics, NLP, and social computing. Her interests include hate‑speech and deception detection, multilingual corpora design, and harm‑aware text analytics for public benefit. This project builds on her experience in corpus construction and responsible AI for safer digital communication.

Prof. Chi‑Bin Cheng

Collaborator

Chi‑Bin Cheng collaborates on multilingual language technologies and peace‑tech applications. His work spans AI‑assisted text analysis, human‑AI interaction, and metrics for evaluating the emotional impact of online language. He contributes to corpus design, evaluation protocols, and public‑facing visualisations.
