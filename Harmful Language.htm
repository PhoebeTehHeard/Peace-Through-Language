Harmful Language: Definitions & Categories

Harmful language refers to any expression that causes psychological, emotional, social, or physical harm to individuals or groups. It can include insults, threats, discrimination, or any form of degrading communication. Categorising harmful language helps in moderation, analysis, and developing safer online environments.

1. Abusive / Insulting Language

Language intended to belittle or demean an individual, without targeting protected characteristics.

Example: “You’re so useless; nobody wants you on this team.”

Key Features: Personal insults, name-calling, belittling, not identity-related.

2. Hate Speech (Targeting Protected Characteristics)

Hostile or derogatory language directed at individuals because of protected traits such as race, ethnicity, religion, gender, disability, sexual orientation, etc.

Example: “People from that ethnic group are not welcome here.”

Key Features: Identity-based attack, generalisations, exclusion, discrimination.

3. Dehumanisation

Language that describes a person or group as less than human, comparing them to animals, objects, or sub-human beings.

Example: “They’re like animals; they don’t deserve rights.”

Key Features: Removal of dignity, comparisons to animals/objects, justification for harm.
